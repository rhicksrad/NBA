Inside the Hub

The full story behind the NBA Intelligence Hub project.

This space chronicles how the entire multi-page experience—from previews and history deep dives to player dossiers and data tooling—came together. Every line of copy and code across the project was created by Ryan Hicks with the assistance of OpenAI Codex, and the About page now reflects the broader vision beyond a single-season preview.

How the project came together

The Hub is a cohesive storytelling laboratory: part data pipeline, part design system, and part long-form magazine. Below is the framework that keeps every page connected.

Creative direction & authorship

Ryan Hicks defined the tone, reporting cadence, and user journeys across the site, using OpenAI Codex as a co-creator to draft copy, wrangle data, and prototype layouts.

  • Unified editorial voice across previews, history, and insights
  • Codex-assisted iteration for narrative structures and polish
  • Single-author accountability for quality and accuracy

Technical architecture

A static-first build with modular HTML, CSS, and scripted data ingestion powers fast loads and maintainable updates without heavyweight frameworks.

  • Reusable component styling in styles/
  • Dataset automation via Python notebooks and scripts
  • Accessible-by-default markup and semantic structure

Research & storytelling engine

Season previews, historical retrospectives, player dossiers, and data experiments all flow from a shared library of stats, league context, and scenario modeling.

  • Cross-era comparisons and trend analysis
  • Playstyle tagging for team and player pages
  • Scenario planners linking insights to future updates

How the story flows from data to drama

The Intelligence Hub runs on a repeatable workflow: establish the league pulse, layer in archival perspective, then surface actionable insights for fans, analysts, and creators.

  1. Scan the league pulse. Dashboards surface roster churn, chemistry grades, and evolving game plans across the regular season and historical archives.
  2. Deep-dive context. History archives and Insights Lab experiments layer precedent, comps, and film study nuggets right beside the metrics.
  3. Decide and share. Export season scenarios, send callouts to collaborators, or embed charts into your preview show rundown.
