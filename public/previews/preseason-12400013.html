Preseason preview Monday, October 07, 2024 — 23:30 UTC

Oklahoma City Thunder at San Antonio Spurs

Frost Bank Center · San Antonio, TX · Preseason opener

Two of the league’s youngest contenders square off with a focus on lead guard reps and frontcourt experimentation.

Why it matters in October

Oklahoma City continues to expand its ball-handling committee. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander will make a cameo, then pass things to Jalen Williams, Cason Wallace, and newcomer Alex Caruso to test different lineup wrinkles. Rookie big Bobi Klintman will also see time as Mark Daigneault experiments with five-out spacing.

San Antonio is handing the keys to sophomore point-forward Jeremy Sochan less this year thanks to No. 4 pick Stephon Castle. Gregg Popovich wants Castle to run the half-court with Victor Wembanyama setting the tone in limited minutes. Rookie guard Juan Nunez and second-year wing Malaki Branham are also in the evaluation window.

The Spurs are managing Devin Vassell’s workload after a minor ankle tweak, while the Thunder are still without Kenrich Williams (back rehab). Both teams are otherwise healthy and looking to harden habits on defense.

  • How quickly does Castle adjust to NBA physicality guarding Thunder guards?
  • Can Wallace and Caruso provide enough secondary creation to free Jalen Williams offensively?
  • What frontcourt combinations around Wembanyama give San Antonio the best rebounding balance?
